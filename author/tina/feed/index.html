]]> Black Rice with veggies, shrimp and zucchini is a light, colorful and flavorful first course that is simple to make and perfect for a summer lunch or buffet, an outing, in short, for all occasions! This tasty and delicate dish, rich in nutritional properties thanks to the Venus rice, is made with shrimp, peas, cashews and your favorite vegetables, everyone really likes it, and it is also very beautiful to look at!

Venus rice contains anthocyanins, flavonols and phenolic acids, phenolic compounds known to have antioxidant properties; contains insoluble fiber, vitamins and minerals such as selenium, iron, calcium, zinc and manganese, it also has low sugar

Ingredients for 3/4 persons

  • 300 gr Black Venus Rice
  • 100 gr Frozen Shrimps
  • one zucchini
  • half a leek
  • 200 gr frozen peas
  • two tomatoes
  • 100 gr roasted cashews
  • to taste Extra-virgin olive oil
  • to taste Salt, pepper
  • a tablespoon of soy sauce
  • fresh basil

First begin by cooking the Venus Rice. Bring plenty of water to a boil, salt it and pour in the black rice. Follow the cooking time indicated on your Venus Rice package, about 40 to 45 minutes, then drain the rice and set aside.

Boil the frozen shrimp for 5/6 minutes, then set aside; do the same with the peas, which you will boil 10 minutes.

Brown the chopped leek in a wok-type pan with two tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil.

Add the chopped zucchini and cook, over medium heat, for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Season with salt and pepper.

Also add the chopped tomatoes, shrimp, peas and finally cashews; add the soy sauce and let it evaporate.

Finally add the rice, stir and serve the venus rice directly in the pot with a few leaves of fresh basil, you will smell it!

]]> Cantonese Rice is the best-known recipe in Chinese cuisine, along with steamed dumplings, spring rolls, and chicken with almonds! This recipe is based on basmati rice first boiled (best the day before!) and then stir-fried in a pan or Wok with scrambled eggs, peas, ham and gaberetti! A simple, quick, tasty and colorful dish, the whole family likes it and the kids love it! Prepare Cantonese rice and serve it with the famous chopsticks, just Chinese style! Ingredients for two/three people:

  • 1 tablespoon Chinese wine
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons seed oil
  • 2 spring onions
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 200 g basmati or Jasmine rice
  • 2 eggs
  • 150 g frozen peas
  • 150 gr fresh or frozen shrimps
  • 150 gr diced cooked ham
  • salt to taste.

To prepare Cantonese rice, first rinse the rice under running water several times until the water is clear (this is a crucial step!), then let it drain on the colander. Cook the rice in plenty of lightly salted water (don’t overdo it because then the soy sauce will take care of it!) for 6 to 7 minutes and drain when al dente. Let the rice cool completely for about 20 to 30 minutes inside a large baking dish-it will reach the right consistency.

Ideally, cook the rice the day before and leave it in the refrigerator overnight-this step ensures the perfect texture of Cantonese rice once saut√©ed!

Mix the Chinese wine, oyster sauce, and soy sauce in a small bowl and set aside (you can find all these ingredients in ethnic stores!) Boil the frozen peas for about 8 minutes, then drain and set aside; do the same thing with the shrimp for 5 minutes, if you got them frozen. In a nonstick skillet or wok, heat 1 tablespoon of seed oil, add the lightly beaten eggs with a pinch of salt, place over medium heat and stir with a whisk a few minutes, then set aside. In the same clean skillet with a sheet of paper towels heat 1 tablespoon of seed oil, add the thinly sliced spring onions and garlic powder, heat 1 minute, then add the cooked peas and shrimp and saute a few minutes.

Add the scrambled eggs, cold rice, and add the sauces, stir to coat the rice and cook over high heat for 2-3 minutes until the liquid evaporates, then turn off the heat. Finally add the cooked ham and let it mix for a few more seconds (the ham should not cook!). Add another tablespoon of oyster or soy sauce if desired! Here’s your Cantonese Rice ready, colorful and flavorful, serve it with the appropriate chopsticks, just Chinese style!

]]> Colored Vol-au-vents are baskets made from several overlapping layers of puff pastry, brushed with egg and baked in the oven, then filled with soft pea cream and boiled quail eggs, or with mayonnaise, lumpfish eggs and smoked salmon. A must on holiday tables, beautiful to look at and easy to prepare, these canestrini are served as an appetizer or finger food, and can be stuffed in a variety of ways, from sweet to savory. The filling inside them is usually based on meat, fish, vegetables, cheese, and mushrooms; however, the dough from which the vol au vent are made has a neutral taste, which can also be combined with sweet preparations, with creams and fruit. With this filling made with peas, mayonnaise, salmon and eggs you will look great for a buffet with friends or for a family Holiday appetizer!

The name “Vol au vent” comes from the fact that they are light enough to be carried “away on the wind.” Their origin is attributed to French chef Marie-Antoine Car√™me who allegedly substituted a heavy crust for the light dough. However, in Fran√ßois Marin’s 1739 cookbook “Les Dons de Comus,” which predates Antoine Car√™me’s birth, a sweet dessert called “wind pie” is mentioned.

Ingredients for 10 vol-au-vents:

  • 2 puff pastry disks
  • 1 egg for brushing
  • 10 quail eggs
  • 150 g frozen peas
  • 2/3 basil leaves
  • 4/5 slices of smoked salmon
  • mayonnaise to taste
  • 5 lumpfish eggs
  • salt and pepper

To prepare the filled vol-au-vents, roll out the first puff pastry and cut out disks with a pastry cutter; proceed with the second puff pastry as well. Place the resulting discs on a baking sheet lined with baking paper and brush them with egg, while cupping the center of the remaining discs with a smaller pastry cutter to create many crowns.

Prick the whole discs so that they do not swell excessively in baking, then lay the pierced discs on top of the large discs, 3 each, also ‘brushed with egg.

Bake in a preheated static oven at 200¬∫ for about 12 to 14 minutes; take out of the oven and let them cool completely.

If the bottom has risen a bit after baking, you can gently squish it down with a teaspoon!

For the filling of the vol-au-vents, boil the peas in lightly salted water, then whisk them together with the basil, a teaspoon of oil, salt and pepper and a tablespoon of cooking water until creamy. For hard-boiled quail eggs, on the other hand, you need to boil them in water for 3-4 minutes from the time they first boil. It’s time to fill your vol-au-vents: so use the pea cream and fill 5, then add one egg each and a sprinkling of oregano.

For salmon vol-au-vents, use mayonnaise, salmon and the lumpfish eggs.

Serve your colorful vol-au-vents!

Pot roast veal is one of the most beloved dishes in Italian cuisine, especially for holidays, such as Easter coming up, for Sunday family meals or special occasions. Its tender and juicy meat is perfect with seasonal vegetables, chips or mashed potatoes: a perfect match with its gravy! Ingredients for 4 people

  • 1 kg of veal (butternut, rump or rump) possibly already tied with string by your butcher
  • 1 glass of White wine
  • 2 glasses of meat stock (or water and stock cube)
  • 3 sprigs of Rosemary
  • carrot
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove Garlic
  • 3 tablespoons of Extra virgin olive oil
  • to tasteSalt
  • to tastePepper

Start the preparation by chopping the vegetables, onion, carrot and garlic, heat the oil in a large pot and saut√© for 5 minutes. Add the veal roast and brown it well on all sides, then deglaze with the wine. Adjust the salt and pepper and add the rosemary.

After adding the white wine, turn the roast several times to give it flavor, then add the meat stock, lower the heat to low. Now cover the pot with a lid and let the veal roast cook for about an hour. After an hour has passed, remove the lid, raise the flame and let the cooking liquid evaporate a little.

To adjust with cooking time: calculate about 55-60 minutes for each pound of meat!

After cooking, arrange the veal roast on a plate. Remove the rosemary sprig from the pot and whisk the cooking juices using a food processor; in this way, the whipped cooking juices serve as a sauce to accompany the veal roast, after cutting it into slices, which you can reheat at the time.

Serve your veal roast with your favorite vegetables!

If you want to get more out of the cooking juices, you need to whisk it all up, put it back in the pot and add a few ladles of water. ]]> Risotto with sausage and zucchini is a tasty, flavorful, creamy and very easy to prepare first course that contrasts the strong flavor of sausage combined with the more delicate flavor of zucchini. Perfect for both lunch and dinner, nutritious and tasty, suitable for all seasons of the year; ideal for Sunday lunch with the family and the kids love it too!
Ingredients for 4 people:
  • 350 g Carnaroli or Vialone nano rice
  • 250 gr of Sausage
  • 3-4 zucchini
  • about 1 l ofpreviously prepared meat broth
  • 1 glass of white wine
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 1 sprig of parsley
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2-3 tablespoons Extra-virgin olive oil
  • butter and grated Grana Padano cheese for creaming

Start the preparation of Risotto with Sausage and Zucchini from the meat stock, which you can also use for other preparations, and keep it warm. In a large saucepan, saut√© the thinly sliced onion in the oil for 3 minutes, then add the sausage stripped of its casing and crumbled with a fork; let it season for a few minutes while keeping the flame fairly high and stirring with a spoon. Add the rice and toast it for 3-4 minutes over high heat, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon. Deglaze with the white wine and let it evaporate. At this point, add the zucchini previously cut into cubes or strips, adjust the salt and pepper, and season to taste. Lower the heat and cook the rice by adding a few ladles of hot broth to cover the rice, let it cook and then add more broth a little at a time, only when the risotto begins to dry out. Cook the risotto for the time indicated, about 13 to 15 minutes. When it is almost cooked, turn up the heat in order to evaporate the excess broth, then season with parsley and more pepper and cream with butter and grated cheese. Serve piping hot and enjoy!
