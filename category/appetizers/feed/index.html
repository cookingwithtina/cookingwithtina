]]> Colored Vol-au-vents are baskets made from several overlapping layers of puff pastry, brushed with egg and baked in the oven, then filled with soft pea cream and boiled quail eggs, or with mayonnaise, lumpfish eggs and smoked salmon. A must on holiday tables, beautiful to look at and easy to prepare, these canestrini are served as an appetizer or finger food, and can be stuffed in a variety of ways, from sweet to savory. The filling inside them is usually based on meat, fish, vegetables, cheese, and mushrooms; however, the dough from which the vol au vent are made has a neutral taste, which can also be combined with sweet preparations, with creams and fruit. With this filling made with peas, mayonnaise, salmon and eggs you will look great for a buffet with friends or for a family Holiday appetizer!

The name “Vol au vent” comes from the fact that they are light enough to be carried “away on the wind.” Their origin is attributed to French chef Marie-Antoine Carême who allegedly substituted a heavy crust for the light dough. However, in François Marin’s 1739 cookbook “Les Dons de Comus,” which predates Antoine Carême’s birth, a sweet dessert called “wind pie” is mentioned.

Ingredients for 10 vol-au-vents:

  • 2 puff pastry disks
  • 1 egg for brushing
  • 10 quail eggs
  • 150 g frozen peas
  • 2/3 basil leaves
  • 4/5 slices of smoked salmon
  • mayonnaise to taste
  • 5 lumpfish eggs
  • salt and pepper

To prepare the filled vol-au-vents, roll out the first puff pastry and cut out disks with a pastry cutter; proceed with the second puff pastry as well. Place the resulting discs on a baking sheet lined with baking paper and brush them with egg, while cupping the center of the remaining discs with a smaller pastry cutter to create many crowns.

Prick the whole discs so that they do not swell excessively in baking, then lay the pierced discs on top of the large discs, 3 each, also ‘brushed with egg.

Bake in a preheated static oven at 200º for about 12 to 14 minutes; take out of the oven and let them cool completely.

If the bottom has risen a bit after baking, you can gently squish it down with a teaspoon!

For the filling of the vol-au-vents, boil the peas in lightly salted water, then whisk them together with the basil, a teaspoon of oil, salt and pepper and a tablespoon of cooking water until creamy. For hard-boiled quail eggs, on the other hand, you need to boil them in water for 3-4 minutes from the time they first boil. It’s time to fill your vol-au-vents: so use the pea cream and fill 5, then add one egg each and a sprinkling of oregano.

For salmon vol-au-vents, use mayonnaise, salmon and the lumpfish eggs.

Serve your colorful vol-au-vents!

]]> Puff Pastry Magics are tasty appetizers, yummy finger foods that are quick and easy! They are made with rectangular and round puff pastry rolls, filled in the center with with tomato sauce, vegetables, cheese, sliced meats…whatever you want! These appetizers can be squares, rolls or small croissants and are ideal for aperitifs, appetizers, buffets, parties and snacks! Make puff pastry magic when you need a quick and easy appetizer that can be made even at the last minute, or for special occasions such as Christmas or Easter with the family, maybe together with a glass of chilled Prosecco! Delicious cold and even the next day, you can even bag them and transport them to the beach or for trips out of town! Ingredients for Savory Croissants

  • 1 roll of round puff pastry
  • one package of philadelphia-type cheese
  • a package of speck
  • sesame seeds
  • milk or egg yolk

Ingredients for the Pizzettas

  • 1 roll of rectangular puff pastry
  • 4/5 tablespoons of tomato sauce
  • one teaspoon oil
  • one teaspoon of tomato paste
  • salt and pepper
  • half a teaspoon of sugar
  • oregano

Ingredients for Artichoke and ham appetizers

  • 2 rolls of rectangular puff pastry
  • 200 g drained artichokes in oil
  • a tablespoon of olive oil
  • a pinch of salt
  • a package of cooked ham
  • milk or egg yolk

Ingredients for Frankfurter Rolls

  • 1 roll of round puff pastry
  • sesame seeds
  • egg yolk or milk
  • 2 packages of frankfurters

To make the Savory Cornets unroll the puff pastry and roll it out in its baking paper, then divide it into four parts, then each into three parts, to make twelve wedges. Spread a teaspoon of philadelphia on all the wedges, and add a slice of bacon (I removed the fattest part).

Fold the savory croissants back up, starting with the base: roll it on itself until you reach the tip of the triangle. Lay the newly made savory croissants on a baking sheet lined with baking paper; brush the surface with egg yolk or milk and sprinkle with a few sesame seeds. Bake your Savory Croissants in a static oven at 200° for 15 to 18 minutes, then take them out of the oven and let them cool. To make the Pizzette , prepare the tomato sauce by heating the oil, sauce, paste, salt and sugar for a few minutes, then let cool Unroll a rectangular roll of puff pastry directly into a baking dish, cut with a knife along vertical and then horizontal strips to make about 2-inch squares; put a teaspoon of tomato sauce in the center, then close two corners in the middle and add oregano.

Bake in a static oven at 200° for 12 to 15 minutes, then take out of the oven and let cool. To make the Artichoke and Ham Stuzzichini, first prepare the artichoke cream by whisking the drained artichokes together with the ‘oil and salt until smooth. Unroll the first roll of puff pastry, add the cream the artichokes perfectly over the entire surface, add the well-spread slices of cooked ham and the second layer of puff pastry. With a well-sharpened knife carve a grid first all vertical strips, then all horizontal strips, so as to make squares.

Bake the appetizers in the middle part of a static oven preheated to 200° and let them bake for about 15 minutes, then take them out of the oven and let them cool out of the pan and serve.

To make the Frankfurter Rolls roll out the round puff pastry parcel and cut it into 12 strips; cut each frankfurter into 4 pieces and roll each piece of frankfurter into a strip making almost a double turn. Arrange the rolls on a baking sheet with baking paper, brush them with egg yolk or milk, and bake in a preheated oven at 200° for about 12 to 15 minutes until the pastry is golden brown.

Delicious variants for the filling of Croissants, Pizzette or Stuzzichini: you can use cooked ham, salami, speck, crudo, or other cold cuts of your choice; instead of philadelphia and artichokes you can spread some tomato and grana cheese, olive pate, pesto alla genovese, acciunghe pasta, tuna and mayonnaise, pesto and ricotta cheese, ricotta cheese and grana cheese or add to the red and white bases cold cuts, cheeses such as fontina or edamer and vegetables of your choice! You can make pizzas with ricotta, mozzarella or other cheeses..in short really whatever you prefer!

]]> Mushroom and buffalo mozzarella savory pie is a really tasty dish with a rich and intense aroma that is quick and easy to prepare in advance. Perfect at any time of year and on any occasion, this is a great recipe to enjoy at lunch, dinner or during an aperitif with friends, as an alternative to pizza and also perfect for a lunch out celebrating Easter or Easter Monday.
Its rustic flavor and delicious aroma will win you over at the first taste, especially when accompanied by a cold beer!

 

  • 200 gr of 00 flour
  • 100 gr chopped butter
  • 50 ml cold water
  • a pinch of salt
  • 700 gr mixed mushrooms (I used prataioli and cardoncello)
  • 1/2 glass of white wine
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • one clove of garlic
  • parsley
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • one buffalo mozzarella
  • 1 egg
  • 100 ml fresh cream
  • 40 g grated parmesan cheese

 

Start the preparation from the brisee dough: Place the flour in a well on the work surface or in a bowl or in the blender. Cut the well-cold refrigerated butter into pieces and add it to the flour, working it quickly with your hands so that it crumbles; add the cold water and pinch of salt, a little at a time, always kneading with your other hand.

 

 

Continue kneading with your hands until you get a smooth, well-blended dough; form into a round loaf and refrigerate 30 minutes wrapped in plastic wrap.

 

 

Prepare the mushrooms: remove any remaining soil with a small brush or cloth, then slice all the mushrooms. Heat the two tablespoons of oil in a pan with the garlic clove cut in half; add the mushrooms and cook over high heat for 5 minutes, deglazing with the wine. Lower the heat, season with salt and pepper and continue cooking for 20 minutes over medium heat. At the end of cooking, remove the garlic, season with parsley, if necessary let the cooking liquid dry for 5/6 minutes on high heat without a lid, then let cool.

 

 

With a whisk, mix the egg with the cream and grated cheese in a bowl. Add the mushroom mixture and stir to combine.

 

 

Roll out the brisee dough on a sheet of baking paper greased with oil, using a rolling pin, obtaining a sheet about 3 mm. thick; place it with its baking paper inside a 22-cm-diameter mold, prick the base with the tines of a fork and pour the prepared mixture inside.

 

 

Add the lightly squeezed sliced buffalo mozzarella and bake in a static oven preheated to 180° for 40 minutes. When finished baking, unmold the savory pie and let it cool before bringing it to the table.

]]> Beef with tuna sauce is a tasty, fresh, flavorful main course, perfect to enjoy cold for spring and summer lunches and dinners, and it can be prepared in advance. Thin slices of meat (I chose beef rump, but veal is also highly recommended) accompanied by a flavorful sauce made with tuna, mayonnaise and eggs. It is a dish that could be served as an appetizer, also suitable for transporting out of the house for an out-of-town lunch.

 

  • 800 g of beef swivel
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 celery stalk
  • 6-7 black peppercorns
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 1/2 liters of water
  • 1 glass of white wine
  • salt and pepper
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs
  • 150 g tuna in oil
  • 3 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • salt
  • pepper
  • beef broth to dilute

 

Peel and chop the white onion, carrot, and shallot and sauté them in a saucepan with the agio and 3 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil and a pinch of salt until just golden.

 

Also put the beef, salt, and peppercorns in the casserole and pour over all the white wine, let it fade. Then add the water, which should cover everything, cover and cook over moderate heat for at least 1 hour. Use the roasting thermometer, inserting it perfectly in the center of the piece of meat to check the internal temperature: when it reaches 65° it will be just pink and ready to eat.

 

 

At this point, move the meat to a bowl and let it cool completely; you can use the meat stock for other preparations, such as risotto or meat sauce, taking care to set aside a couple of tablespoons for the tonnata sauce.

 

Meanwhile, move on to preparing the tonnata sauce: boil the eggs (when boiling, count 9 minutes of cooking time), then let them cool. In a blender put the hard-boiled yolks, add the drained tuna, mayonnaise, salt and pepper, and finally add a little broth. Blend until smooth and of the consistency you prefer.

 

 

Thinly slice the now-warm meat with a smooth-bladed knife; arrange the slices in a serving dish and pour the resulting cream, making layers with the meat slices: cover with plastic wrap and leave to set in the refrigerator a few hours before serving.

]]> Grilled Eggplant is an easy, light, quick and tasty vegetable side dish made with slices of eggplant grilled and then seasoned with a fragrant marinade of oil, garlic, mint and embellished with olives. This flavorful dish is perfect to pair with any dish, such as grilled meat or fish, with cheeses and cured meats or; you can serve it as an appetizer, to stuff sandwiches or crostini. Also, once consumed, you can use the remaining oil to dress pasta!

 

  • 3 eggplants
  • extra virgin olive oil to taste. (about two cups)
  • 100 g green or black olives, pitted
  • 3/4 cloves of garlic
  • 2 hot peppers
  • coarse salt
  • pepper to taste
  • mint leaves

 

Begin preparation by slicing the eggplant with a long, sharp knife or a mandoline to a thickness of about 4 to 5 mm.

 

 

Heat a grill well, add a little coarse salt and begin grilling the eggplant slices, for about 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until the black stripes typical of well-grilled vegetables appear. As you grill, add a little more coarse salt to the grill.

 

 

Meanwhile, prepare the high-sided baking dish where you will put the eggplant: on the bottom put a little olive oil, a couple of mint leaves, a couple of pieces of fresh garlic, pepper.

 

 

Lay the grilled eggplant slices in the marinade, so that they fill the bottom. Add on top of the eggplant layer some more oil, garlic, olives, pepper, and if you like a chili pepper too!

 

 

Intersperse with more eggplant, and proceed this way, in layers, until you run out of ingredients. Go ahead and abound with the ‘olive oil, then you will reuse it to season the pasta, nothing goes to waste in the kitchen! In the ‘last layer add olives, garlic and mint.

 

Finally let them marinate for at least 30 minutes before serving, even better if you let them sit in the fridge overnight! You can serve your mouthwatering eggplant cold as well!

 

You can alternatively use a glass jar, always proceeding in layers, perfect for gifting eggplant to friends and family!

]]> Taiwanese style Shrimps (in Italy called “in Guazzetto”) is a tasty dish, simple and quick to prepare, with aromas and flavors typical of Taiwanese cuisine, rich in spices and seasonings such as soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, cilantro (also called “Chinese parsley”) and Thai basil; products that can be found in most well-stocked ethnic stores. This colorful, flavorful and seemingly simple dish is instead a ‘set of interesting, unique and sought-after flavors, perfect as an appetizer, main course or main dish along with Basmati or Jasmine rice.

 

  • 20 gr of fresh ginger root
  • 25 gr of sesame oil (alternatively seed oil)
  • 400 gr fresh shrimp tails
  • 30 gr of rice wine or mirin
  • 20 gr of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 60 gr leek

 

Shell and clean the shrimp tails well, also removing the black thread inside, and rinse with cold water.

 

 

In a large frying pan, preferably a wok, heat the sesame oil along with the chopped or grated ginger and flavor for 2 minutes, then add the sliced leek and cook another 2 minutes. Add the cleaned shrimp and cook for 2 to 3 minutes, then pour in the rice wine, raise the heat and cook another 3 minutes.

 

 

Add the soy sauce and sugar and cook, still on high heat, for another 4 minutes, allowing some of the cooking liquid to evaporate.

 

 

Serve your Taiwanese-style Shrimp with Basmati rice, perfect for enjoying the savory sauce!

]]> Yorkshire Pudding is a typical preparation of Anglo-Saxon cuisine, particularly the county of Yorkshire. This fluffy and tasty souffle is served as a side dish and is prepared simply with eggs, milk and flour; but its uniqueness lies in its shape: in fact, it has a “hole” in the center, which is ideal for accommodating sauces, meat cooking juices, such as classic roast beef, or roasts, stews or sausages. Puddings are served at Sunday lunches; traditionally it is baked in the oven under the roast: the fat released in cooking goes to enhance the cooking of the pudding and helps flavor this delicious dish. You can serve them with smoked salmon, or stuffed with pate or anything else you like, such as sliced meats and cheeses, for a truly original brunch!

 

Ingredients for 8 Puddings:

 

  • 400 g Whole Milk
  • 150 gr of 00 flour
  • 80 gr seed oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • a pinch of pepper

 

To prepare the Yorkshire pudding, take two muffin pans and pour the seed oil inside 8 molds, distributing it evenly. Place the pan in the static oven preheated to 200° and heat the oil for about 10 minutes.

 

 

With the Bimby: Pour all other ingredients into the mug and mix 15 seconds/speed 6.

 

 

Without the Bimby: Beat the eggs with the milk, pepper and salt in a large bowl. Gradually add the flour, stirring with a whisk until the ‘mixture is smooth and lump-free.

 

Pour the batter into the hot muffin pan (be careful not to burn yourself with the hot oil) and bake again at 200° for 25 minutes, never opening the oven. Once baked, the muffin tarts will look nice and puffy and then tend to deflate to their characteristic irregular shape-let them cool slightly before unmolding and serving your Yorkshire pudding!

 

 

 

Yorkshire pudding can also be enjoyed cold, with sliced meats and cheeses; the center hole can then be used to insert your favorite ingredients. Yorkshire pudding can also be used as an alternative to bread, and since it has a rather neutral taste, it is perfect to try with jam or sweet cream.

]]> Blinis are a tasty and original appetizer, a typical dish throughout Russia; a kind of soft pancake made with buckwheat flour. These savory buns are served mostly during the “Masleinza” (Pancake) festival, which follows the cold winter. Once ready, Blinis are enriched with melted butter, sour cream, smoked salmon, dill and caviar, or whatever you prefer, even blueberry jam!

 

Even the famous Russian poet Alexander Kuprin (1870-1938) wrote a few lines dedicating it to this traditional dish: “Blinis are round, warm and gorgeous like the sun. They are actually the symbol of the sun, of peaceful days, wedding lunches, healthy children.”

 

Ingredients for 9 Blinis:

 

  • 200 gr of Milk
  • 40 g of 00 flour
  • a pinch of sugar
  • one egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 100 gr buckwheat flour
  • 20 gr fresh brewer’s yeast, or a sachet of the dry kind
  • seed oil for baking

 

To garnish:

 

  • Smoked salmon
  • Smoked tuna
  • 40 g melted butter
  • dill
  • jams or compotes

 

With the Bimby: Pour the milk, brewer’s yeast and sugar into the mug and heat slightly for 1 minute 30 seconds/37°/ speed 1. Add 00 flour, buckwheat flour, egg, half teaspoon salt and pepper: 30 seconds/speed 4.

 

 

Without the Bimby: Warm the milk in a small saucepan, then turn off and melt the yeast together with the sugar; in a separate bowl mix the two flours, add the milk at a trickle, continuing to stir with a whisk to prevent lumps from forming. Then add the egg, salt and pepper and mix until the dough is smooth and rather liquid.

 

Let the dough rest and rise for about an hour in a dry place.

 

 

Heat and grease a nonstick pan for crepes with a little seed oil; when it is hot, place a couple of ladles of dough in it, which will swell to form crepes. When the bottom has firmed up turn the blinis over with the help of a spatula and brown the other side as well. Continue in this way until all the mixture is finished.

 

 

Decorate the blinis: spread a little melted butter, place a slice of smoked salmon on top and garnish with dill. The blinis are ready to be enjoyed for a great appetizer!

 

 

You can serve the blinis with sour cream or cream cheese (like philadelphia) or Greek yogurt instead of melted butter, and replace the salmon with shrimp, ham, caviar, or lumpfish roe. The blinis can also be served as a dessert at the end of a meal: just accompany them with jam or hazelnut cream!

  • 10 g fresh ginger
  • 50 gr chopped carrots
  • 30 gr of cooked ham
  • 300 gr chopped pork
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a teaspoon of sesame oil (alternatively seed oil)
  • 400 gr of water
  • one tablespoon of soy sauce
  • one teaspoon of granulated stock cube
  • 10 gr of cornstarch
  • 20 gr rice vinegar or mirim or sake
  • 2 sliced spring onions

 

With the Bimby: put the ginger in the mug and mince 2 seconds/speed 8, then bring together with the spatula

 

Add the carrots and cooked ham and chop 5 seconds/speed 5

 

 

If you do not have a Bimby, you can use an ordinary food processor.

 

Add the pork, salt, pepper, oil and blend 2 minutes/speed 2

 

Without the Bimby, transfer the meat to a large bowl, add the chopped vegetables and mix the mixture with your hands.

 

 

Form into balls of about 15 g each and spread them on the Varoma dish; if you do not have the Bimby, any steamer will do.

 

Pour the water into the mug, place the Varoma with the meatballs and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. While cooking, mix the rice vinegar with the cornstarch without forming lumps.

 

 

 

Remove the Varoma, add the stock cube and soy sauce to the mug and thicken 2 min/Varoma/speed 2, pouring in the cornstarch with the wine vinegar from the hole in the lid.

 

Add the sliced spring onions and cook 2 minutes/Varoma/speed 1.

 

If you do not have a Bimby, after steaming the meatballs (always 15 minutes) you can prepare the sauce in a small saucepan with the same ingredients and cook until smooth and thick.

 

Transfer the Chinese meatballs to a serving plate, add the thick sauce on top and serve with basmati rice and soy sauce!

]]> The batter-fried squash blossoms are a tasty, crispy appetizer suitable for any occasion and impossible to resist. Perfect for dinner as an appetizer, or at cocktail hour, fried squash blossoms are super easy and quick to prepare, and everyone really likes them.

 

  • 8 squash blossoms
  • 100 g 00 flour
  • 1 egg
  • 120 ml milk
  • salt, pepper to taste
  • seed oil for frying

 

Start by preparing the pumpkin flower batter: work the egg with a pinch of salt and pepper with a whisk, add first the milk then the flour and mix until smooth, soft and without lumps. Let the batter rest in the refrigerator for 30 minutes, covered with plastic wrap.

 

 

Meanwhile, take care of cleaning the squash blossoms: gently detach the stem, outer pistils and inner bud; repeat the same operation for all the other squash blossoms. Wash them gently in cold water, drain and let them dry.

 

 

Pour the oil into a pan and bring it to a temperature of 160-170° maximum (get a kitchen thermometer to do this); then dip the flowers into the batter and using tongs turn them over so that they are entirely covered. Then remove each flower from the batter, letting the excess drip off, and dip a few pieces at a time into the hot oil

 

 

When they are nicely browned, turn them over and continue cooking them on the other side (a few minutes will suffice); using a skimmer, remove them from the oil and place them on a sheet of paper towels. Season with a pinch of salt and serve your battered squash blossoms.

 

 

Want an ‘alternative to classic batter? You can add some dry brewer’s yeast (5/6 g) or prepare it with 100 g of flour and 200 ml of light beer, or tempura-like with 200 ml of cold carbonated water, 100 g of flour and an egg!
