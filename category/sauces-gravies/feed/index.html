]]> The cocktail sauce, also known as pink sauce, is a very tasty sauce with a delicate and light flavor, very quick and easy to prepare, made from mayonnaise, ketchup and the cognac or brandy, to which mustard, Worcestershire sauce and, in some cases, some fresh cooking cream or yogurt are then added. Pink sauce is ideal for accompanying fish and shellfish dishes, and in particular it is used in the preparation of shrimp cocktail. You can use this tasty sauce to create tempting appetizers, or add flavor to sandwiches and sandwiches as well.

 

Serves 4:

 

  • 300 g of Mayonnaise
  • 80 g of Ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon of Worcestershire Sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Cognac or Brandy or 2 tablespoons white wine
  • 1 teaspoon of Mustard (optional)

 

First make homemade mayonnaise following this recipe, or buy it already made!

 

Put the mayonnaise in a bowl and add the ketchup, cognac or brandy or wine and worcestershire sauce and mix well with a spoon so that a smooth sauce forms.

 

 

At this point your cocktail sauce is ready to use; you can put it in small bowls together with blanched shrimp or as an accompaniment to second fish.

 

 

If you want to make the rose sauce with fresh cooking cream, here’s how to do it: lightly whip the cream in a bowl. In another bowl mix all the other ingredients, as per the recipe, and add the cream to the mixture. For a lighter preparation you can substitute the cream with low-fat yogurt or Greek yogurt, eliminating the mustard.

]]> Burger sauce is a flavorful sauce made from mayonnaise, ketchup and mustard, made special by a base of onion and gherkins and flavored with the tangy tang of Worcestershire sauce. This sauce is a real treat, perfect for adding flavor to your homemade hamburgers or for topping fries, in perfect USA style!

 

  • 120 g mayonnaise
  • 50 gr of ketchup
  • 15 gr of mustard
  • one teaspoon tabasco sauce
  • one teaspoon of Worcestershire sauce
  • half of pureed chopped onion (or onion powder)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 4/5 pickled gherkins, chopped into small pieces

 

To make the Hamburger Sauce, put all the ingredients in a bowl in this order: 120 g mayonnaise, the Tabasco, ketchup, mustard, a few drops of Worcestershire sauce, chopped onion, the garlic, and the sliced gherkins. Mix everything together well until the sauce is smooth and free of lumps.

 

 

Transfer the sauce to a minute jar with a lid and let it sit for about half an hour in the refrigerator before using it so that the flavors blend well.

 

Use your Burger sauce to stuff your homemade Hamburger!

]]> Caramelized Onions are a simple, quick, inexpensive and delicious recipe, perfect to accompany aged cheeses, meat main courses, or to stuff crostini, sandwiches and gourmet burgers! The ingredients you need are few and simple: just onions, vinegar and sugar and you’re done! Caramelized onions have a slight sweet and sour taste and a really delicious melting texture! Use this recipe to stuff your homemade hamburger, and also try my recipe for sweet and sour onions!

 

  • 4 medium white onions
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup or so of balsamic vinegar
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • two juniper berries

 

To prepare the caramelized onions, start by peeling them, removing both the skin and the two ends. Then move on to cutting: divide them in half, then slice them to a thickness of about 1 centimeter.

 

Pour the sliced onions into a large saucepan along with the ‘oil, granulated sugar, pepper and salt; mix well and add the water.

 

Cover with a lid and cook over moderate heat for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

 

 

Uncover and continue cooking for another 30 minutes; always over moderate heat and stirring occasionally

 

After the 30 minutes have elapsed, raise the flame and deglaze with the balsamic vinegar, let the alcoholic part of the vinegar evaporate quickly and mix everything together.

 

 

Finish cooking over high heat, letting the onions dry out well, until the right consistency (thick) is achieved.

 

 

Turn off and let cool before serving.
