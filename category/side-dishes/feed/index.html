]]> Caramelized Onions are a simple, quick, inexpensive and delicious recipe, perfect to accompany aged cheeses, meat main courses, or to stuff crostini, sandwiches and gourmet burgers! The ingredients you need are few and simple: just onions, vinegar and sugar and you’re done! Caramelized onions have a slight sweet and sour taste and a really delicious melting texture! Use this recipe to stuff your homemade hamburger, and also try my recipe for sweet and sour onions!

 

  • 4 medium white onions
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 1/2 cup or so of balsamic vinegar
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • two juniper berries

 

To prepare the caramelized onions, start by peeling them, removing both the skin and the two ends. Then move on to cutting: divide them in half, then slice them to a thickness of about 1 centimeter.

 

Pour the sliced onions into a large saucepan along with the ‘oil, granulated sugar, pepper and salt; mix well and add the water.

 

Cover with a lid and cook over moderate heat for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

 

 

Uncover and continue cooking for another 30 minutes; always over moderate heat and stirring occasionally

 

After the 30 minutes have elapsed, raise the flame and deglaze with the balsamic vinegar, let the alcoholic part of the vinegar evaporate quickly and mix everything together.

 

 

Finish cooking over high heat, letting the onions dry out well, until the right consistency (thick) is achieved.

 

 

Turn off and let cool before serving.

]]> Grilled Eggplant is an easy, light, quick and tasty vegetable side dish made with slices of eggplant grilled and then seasoned with a fragrant marinade of oil, garlic, mint and embellished with olives. This flavorful dish is perfect to pair with any dish, such as grilled meat or fish, with cheeses and cured meats or; you can serve it as an appetizer, to stuff sandwiches or crostini. Also, once consumed, you can use the remaining oil to dress pasta!

 

  • 3 eggplants
  • extra virgin olive oil to taste. (about two cups)
  • 100 g green or black olives, pitted
  • 3/4 cloves of garlic
  • 2 hot peppers
  • coarse salt
  • pepper to taste
  • mint leaves

 

Begin preparation by slicing the eggplant with a long, sharp knife or a mandoline to a thickness of about 4 to 5 mm.

 

 

Heat a grill well, add a little coarse salt and begin grilling the eggplant slices, for about 2 to 3 minutes on each side, or until the black stripes typical of well-grilled vegetables appear. As you grill, add a little more coarse salt to the grill.

 

 

Meanwhile, prepare the high-sided baking dish where you will put the eggplant: on the bottom put a little olive oil, a couple of mint leaves, a couple of pieces of fresh garlic, pepper.

 

 

Lay the grilled eggplant slices in the marinade, so that they fill the bottom. Add on top of the eggplant layer some more oil, garlic, olives, pepper, and if you like a chili pepper too!

 

 

Intersperse with more eggplant, and proceed this way, in layers, until you run out of ingredients. Go ahead and abound with the ‘olive oil, then you will reuse it to season the pasta, nothing goes to waste in the kitchen! In the ‘last layer add olives, garlic and mint.

 

Finally let them marinate for at least 30 minutes before serving, even better if you let them sit in the fridge overnight! You can serve your mouthwatering eggplant cold as well!

 

You can alternatively use a glass jar, always proceeding in layers, perfect for gifting eggplant to friends and family!

]]> Green beans with cashews are a great savory side dish, perfect for enjoying this summer vegetable at its best, with an ethnic twist thanks to the ‘addition of curry and cashews. You can serve this dish as a side dish along with ethnic dishes such as chicken cur ry or shrimp curry, or with simple Basmati rice and soy sauce, for an original vegetarian dinner!

 

  • one teaspoon of garlic powder
  • one teaspoon of curry powder
  • 30 g of leek
  • two dried chilies
  • 20 gr peanut or seed oil
  • 300 gr green beans cleaned and cut into small pieces
  • half a glass of water
  • 50 gr roasted and salted cashews
  • salt and pepper to taste

 

Heat the peanut oil in a large pot, add the chopped leek, chili, curry powder and garlic powder and cook for 3 minutes. Add the chopped green beans, which have been previously washed and cleaned of the prickles, also add the water, salt and pepper and cook with the lid on for about 20 minutes, until the green beans are soft.

 

 

Add the cashews as well, remove the lid and finish cooking over medium heat for 5 minutes.

 

 

Serve the Thai-style green beans with a meat main course or Basmati rice pe a vegetarian dinner!

]]> Yorkshire Pudding is a typical preparation of Anglo-Saxon cuisine, particularly the county of Yorkshire. This fluffy and tasty souffle is served as a side dish and is prepared simply with eggs, milk and flour; but its uniqueness lies in its shape: in fact, it has a “hole” in the center, which is ideal for accommodating sauces, meat cooking juices, such as classic roast beef, or roasts, stews or sausages. Puddings are served at Sunday lunches; traditionally it is baked in the oven under the roast: the fat released in cooking goes to enhance the cooking of the pudding and helps flavor this delicious dish. You can serve them with smoked salmon, or stuffed with pate or anything else you like, such as sliced meats and cheeses, for a truly original brunch!

 

Ingredients for 8 Puddings:

 

  • 400 g Whole Milk
  • 150 gr of 00 flour
  • 80 gr seed oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • a pinch of pepper

 

To prepare the Yorkshire pudding, take two muffin pans and pour the seed oil inside 8 molds, distributing it evenly. Place the pan in the static oven preheated to 200° and heat the oil for about 10 minutes.

 

 

With the Bimby: Pour all other ingredients into the mug and mix 15 seconds/speed 6.

 

 

Without the Bimby: Beat the eggs with the milk, pepper and salt in a large bowl. Gradually add the flour, stirring with a whisk until the ‘mixture is smooth and lump-free.

 

Pour the batter into the hot muffin pan (be careful not to burn yourself with the hot oil) and bake again at 200° for 25 minutes, never opening the oven. Once baked, the muffin tarts will look nice and puffy and then tend to deflate to their characteristic irregular shape-let them cool slightly before unmolding and serving your Yorkshire pudding!

 

 

 

Yorkshire pudding can also be enjoyed cold, with sliced meats and cheeses; the center hole can then be used to insert your favorite ingredients. Yorkshire pudding can also be used as an alternative to bread, and since it has a rather neutral taste, it is perfect to try with jam or sweet cream.

]]> The batter-fried squash blossoms are a tasty, crispy appetizer suitable for any occasion and impossible to resist. Perfect for dinner as an appetizer, or at cocktail hour, fried squash blossoms are super easy and quick to prepare, and everyone really likes them.

 

  • 8 squash blossoms
  • 100 g 00 flour
  • 1 egg
  • 120 ml milk
  • salt, pepper to taste
  • seed oil for frying

 

Start by preparing the pumpkin flower batter: work the egg with a pinch of salt and pepper with a whisk, add first the milk then the flour and mix until smooth, soft and without lumps. Let the batter rest in the refrigerator for 30 minutes, covered with plastic wrap.

 

 

Meanwhile, take care of cleaning the squash blossoms: gently detach the stem, outer pistils and inner bud; repeat the same operation for all the other squash blossoms. Wash them gently in cold water, drain and let them dry.

 

 

Pour the oil into a pan and bring it to a temperature of 160-170° maximum (get a kitchen thermometer to do this); then dip the flowers into the batter and using tongs turn them over so that they are entirely covered. Then remove each flower from the batter, letting the excess drip off, and dip a few pieces at a time into the hot oil

 

 

When they are nicely browned, turn them over and continue cooking them on the other side (a few minutes will suffice); using a skimmer, remove them from the oil and place them on a sheet of paper towels. Season with a pinch of salt and serve your battered squash blossoms.

 

 

Want an ‘alternative to classic batter? You can add some dry brewer’s yeast (5/6 g) or prepare it with 100 g of flour and 200 ml of light beer, or tempura-like with 200 ml of cold carbonated water, 100 g of flour and an egg!

]]> The Hasselback Potatoes, or “accordion” potatoes, are a fragrant, tasty and flavorful side dish typical of Swedish cuisine; a recipe created and made famous by the Stockholm restaurant Hasselbacken, which first presented baked potatoes in this way. A quick and easy dish to prepare, but impressive; perfect as a side dish to accompany dishes of all kinds: meat, fish, eggs or vegetables!

 

Ingredients for two people:

 

  • 4 medium-large potatoes
  • 15 g butter
  • 2 tablespoon parmesan cheese
  • 2 tablespoons chopped rosemary
  • 1 teaspoon of garlic powder
  • 30 gr extra-virgin olive oil
  • salt and pepper to taste.

 

First wash and dry the potatoes well (leave the skin on), then make cuts with a sharp-bladed knife, on the surface of the potatoes, a few millimeters apart, a little more than half as deep as each potato.

 

 

Prepare the filling: put rosemary, grana cheese, garlic, salt and pepper in a small bowl and mix, meanwhile turn on the static oven to 200°C

 

Lay the potatoes inside a baking dish greased with oil, massage them with oil so that it penetrates well and fill the inside of the slices with the herb and cheese filling, you can eventually add what is left over on top.

 

 

Before baking add a knob of butter on top of each potato, bake for 60 to 70 minutes (depends on the size of your potatoes, check for doneness with a toothpick).

 

Remove from the oven, let them cool slightly, then serve them; I accompanied them with a chianina burger and spinach sautéed in butter!

 

 

You can customize the Hasselback potato filling with whatever you like: you can add oregano, thyme, or between slices insert a slice of bacon, bacon, ham or cheese!

]]> La Peperonata è un contorno ricco, gustoso, vegetariano e facilissimo da preparare, a base di pochi e semplici ingredienti che rendono questo piatto davvero genuino, intenso, colorato e saporito. Ideale da servire tiepida o fredda, anche il giorno successivo, la peperonata è perfetta per accompagnare un secondo piatto di carne, un tagliere di formaggi e salumi, oppure servita con riso basmati, come gustoso sugo per condire la pasta, oppure per preparare delle golose bruschette. Esistono diverse varianti di questa ricetta, a seconda della tradizione regionale e dei gusti personali: con l’ aggiunta di peperoncino, capperi, acciughe, patate; io vi propongo quella che preferisco in assoluto!

  • 800 gr di peperoni gialli e rossi
  • 300 gr di passata di pomodoro
  • 4 cucchiai di olio extravergine di oliva
  • un cucchiaio di triplo concentrato di pomodoro (facoltativo)
  • 1 cipolla
  • 1 cucchiaio di vino bianco
  • 1 cucchiaio di aceto di vino bianco
  • sale e pepe q.b.

Lavate e asciugate i peperoni e disponeteli interi su una teglia ricoperta di carta da forno, infornate in forno statico preriscaldato a 180° per 20 minuti, quindi sfornateli e lasciateli intiepidire qualche minuto.

Questa precottura serve per rendere la peperonata molto piu’ digeribile e gustosa!

Spellate i peperoni (la buccia verrà via facilmente), eliminate semi e filamenti interni e tagliateli a strisce abbastanza larghe.

In una padella antiaderente fate scaldare i quattro cucchiai d’olio insieme alla cipolla tagliata a fettine. Dopo un paio di minuti aggiungete i peperoni, il concentrato e la passata di pomodoro e lasciate cuocere a fuoco alto per 5 minuti.

Bagnate con l’aceto e il vino, mescolate bene, lasciate evaporare qualche minuti e condite con il sale e il pepe. Abbassate la fiamma e lasciate cuocere con il coperchio per 40 minuti, gli ultimi 6/7 minuti senza coperchio per farla asciugare bene.

Togliete dal fuoco e servite la peperonata tiepida o anche fredda con qualche fogliolina di basilico.

Personalizzate la vostra peperonata come preferite: potete aggiungere olive verdi o nere, capperi, acciughe, prezzemolo…e ricordate che il giorno dopo è ancora piu’ buona!
