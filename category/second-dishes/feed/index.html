Pot roast veal is one of the most beloved dishes in Italian cuisine, especially for holidays, such as Easter coming up, for Sunday family meals or special occasions. Its tender and juicy meat is perfect with seasonal vegetables, chips or mashed potatoes: a perfect match with its gravy! Ingredients for 4 people

  • 1 kg of veal (butternut, rump or rump) possibly already tied with string by your butcher
  • 1 glass of White wine
  • 2 glasses of meat stock (or water and stock cube)
  • 3 sprigs of Rosemary
  • carrot
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove Garlic
  • 3 tablespoons of Extra virgin olive oil
  • to tasteSalt
  • to tastePepper

Start the preparation by chopping the vegetables, onion, carrot and garlic, heat the oil in a large pot and saut√© for 5 minutes. Add the veal roast and brown it well on all sides, then deglaze with the wine. Adjust the salt and pepper and add the rosemary.

After adding the white wine, turn the roast several times to give it flavor, then add the meat stock, lower the heat to low. Now cover the pot with a lid and let the veal roast cook for about an hour. After an hour has passed, remove the lid, raise the flame and let the cooking liquid evaporate a little.

To adjust with cooking time: calculate about 55-60 minutes for each pound of meat!

After cooking, arrange the veal roast on a plate. Remove the rosemary sprig from the pot and whisk the cooking juices using a food processor; in this way, the whipped cooking juices serve as a sauce to accompany the veal roast, after cutting it into slices, which you can reheat at the time.

Serve your veal roast with your favorite vegetables!

If you want to get more out of the cooking juices, you need to whisk it all up, put it back in the pot and add a few ladles of water. Place back on the heat, add a tablespoon of flour, mixed in a small glass of water. In this way you will get more of the cooking juices, to serve the veal roast that you have cooked in the pot.

]]> Beef with tuna sauce is a tasty, fresh, flavorful main course, perfect to enjoy cold for spring and summer lunches and dinners, and it can be prepared in advance. Thin slices of meat (I chose beef rump, but veal is also highly recommended) accompanied by a flavorful sauce made with tuna, mayonnaise and eggs. It is a dish that could be served as an appetizer, also suitable for transporting out of the house for an out-of-town lunch.

 

  • 800 g of beef swivel
  • 1 onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 celery stalk
  • 6-7 black peppercorns
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 1/2 liters of water
  • 1 glass of white wine
  • salt and pepper
  • 3 hard-boiled eggs
  • 150 g tuna in oil
  • 3 tablespoons of mayonnaise
  • salt
  • pepper
  • beef broth to dilute

 

Peel and chop the white onion, carrot, and shallot and saut√© them in a saucepan with the agio and 3 tablespoons of extra-virgin olive oil and a pinch of salt until just golden.

 

Also put the beef, salt, and peppercorns in the casserole and pour over all the white wine, let it fade. Then add the water, which should cover everything, cover and cook over moderate heat for at least 1 hour. Use the roasting thermometer, inserting it perfectly in the center of the piece of meat to check the internal temperature: when it reaches 65¬∞ it will be just pink and ready to eat.

 

 

At this point, move the meat to a bowl and let it cool completely; you can use the meat stock for other preparations, such as risotto or meat sauce, taking care to set aside a couple of tablespoons for the tonnata sauce.

 

Meanwhile, move on to preparing the tonnata sauce: boil the eggs (when boiling, count 9 minutes of cooking time), then let them cool. In a blender put the hard-boiled yolks, add the drained tuna, mayonnaise, salt and pepper, and finally add a little broth. Blend until smooth and of the consistency you prefer.

 

 

Thinly slice the now-warm meat with a smooth-bladed knife; arrange the slices in a serving dish and pour the resulting cream, making layers with the meat slices: cover with plastic wrap and leave to set in the refrigerator a few hours before serving.

]]> Meatballs are a tasty, quick and easy dish of Italian tradition, prepared in many ways, depending on the location and the ingredients available. The only common element is the use of ground meat, which can belong to different cuts and animals; to this is usually added some wet stale bread or mashed boiled potatoes, and eggs; they are then lightly fried and given flavor in a good sauce, or cooked in a pan or in the oven. In this recipe I used four kinds of meat (veal, chicken, beef and pork), did not add soaked bread but a potato, and cooked them in a pan with white wine. Amazing result! Try my recipe for the four mince meatballs, your children and others will surely love it! If you have leftover broth meat, also try my recipe for boiled meatballs!

 

Doses for 55 meatballs of about 20 gr

 

  • 200 gr ground veal meat
  • 200 gr mixed ground beef and pork
  • 300 gr ground chicken meat
  • one medium egg
  • one medium boiled potato
  • a teaspoon of garlic powder or a clove of minced garlic
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a sprig of chopped parsley
  • Breadcrumbs to taste
  • 1/2 glass of white wine
  • olive oil or seed oil to taste.

 

To prepare the meatballs, start by making the dough: in a large bowl put the ground meat, egg, boiled and mashed potato, salt, pepper, garlic and chopped parsley.

 

 

Mix the ingredients to make a smooth dough, then form into many patties of about 20-22 g each.

 

 

Coat all the patties in breadcrumbs arranged in a small plate and refrigerate until ready to cook (you can prepare them the day before, too!)

 

 

To cook the patties: heat two tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet and arrange the patties spaced apart, cook over medium-high heat two minutes on one side and two minutes on the other side, then lower the heat and deglaze with white wine. Cover with the lid and cook another 5 to 6 minutes, then turn them over and cook another 5 minutes.

 

 

Remove the lid, raise the flame and let it evaporate until cooked.

 

 

Serve your patties with your favorite sauces and a side of fresh or cooked vegetables or some chips!

 

 

If you prefer, you can also fry your patties: so use a deep fryer and bring the frying oil to a temperature that does not exceed 170-180¬∞. When it is hot enough, dip 2-3 meatballs at a time so that the oil temperature does not drop too low, cook for a few minutes (2-3 minutes will be enough) until nicely browned, then transfer the cooked meatballs to paper towels just long enough to wipe off the excess oil.

]]> Plokkfiskur is a traditional Icelandic dish of fish, potatoes, onion, and bechamel sauce. A thick and creamy recipe, it is popular with locals and tourists alike. Fish stew is a rich dish, with butter and milk (or cream), served with thin slices of rye bread. Tasty and flavorful, perfect for a “travel” themed evening! Also try my recipe for Icelandic Cheesecake !

 

Serves two/three people:

 

  • 20 g butter
  • one chopped onion
  • one and a half tablespoons of flour
  • 400 ml whole milk
  • 300 gr fresh cod cut into pieces
  • 300 gr of potatoes
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • slices of whole wheat or rye bread and butter to taste to accompany the dish

 

Start the preparation by peeling the potatoes and cutting them into pieces, then cook them in salted boiling water for about 10-12 minutes, then drain and set aside; in another pot also cook the chopped cod and boil it for about 10 minutes, drain that too.

 

 

Chop the onion well and put it in a large saucepan together with the butter, wilt it on a low heat (do not let it brown too much) for 6/7 minutes, add the flour and mix well until it forms lumps

 

 

Add the milk in two batches and stir continuously, simmer for 5/6 minutes until thick; add the fish and stir again, then add the potatoes and give it a good stir

 

 

If you want to get a softer stew, add a tablespoon or two of milk, and adjust the salt and pepper.

 

Serve the Icelandic stew with slices of buttered rye bread (I also found a flaxseed bread molo similar to the Icelandic one but made in Norway!), just as tradition dictates!

]]> Beef Stroganoff is a creamy, flavorful main course that originated in Russian cuisine: it consists of beef cut into strips and cooked with onion, tomato puree, all of which is tinged with vodka and flavored with smetana cream (or sour cream) and mustard. This hearty and nutritious dish is easy to make and very satisfying; make it if you want to impress your diners!

 

There are two versions about the origin of this particular recipe, which is roughly in the mid-19th century: according to some, the dish was first prepared by a French chef in the service of Russian Count Pavel Stroganoff who, in order to make beef fricassee more palatable to his master, added a sauce made from sour cream; according to the other version, on the other hand, the recipe would take its name from the doctor who cured Tsarina Maria of food poisoning caused by herring with a diet of beef, onions and sour cream.

 

  • 800 gr Beef Meat (tenderloin, sirloin, pink or walnut)
  • 500 gr white onions
  • 50 gr Butter
  • to taste Parsley for chopping
  • q.b. Fine salt and black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 25 g flour
  • 40 gr of tomato puree
  • a small glass of vodka or brandy
  • one tablespoon of mustard
  • 80 gr smetana sauce *

 

* For the smetana sauce (which you can substitute with sour cream):

 

  • 250 ml fresh cream
  • 40 gr of mascarpone cheese
  • juice of half a lemon

 

To prepare beef stroganoff, start by preparing the smetana sauce: just whip the cream, add the mascarpone and the juice of the lemons, then letting it sit for about two hours. You can skip this preparation and use 80 g of sour cream.

 

 

Finely slice the onions and wilt them 20 minutes in a large pot, along with 25 gr butter and a drizzle of oil; then set aside.

 

 

Cut the beef into strips with a well-sharpened knife, flour them and season with salt, pepper and the ‘garlic powder; cook them with a drizzle of oil and the remaining 25 g butter in the same pan over high heat; as soon as they start to brown, deglaze with the vodka or brandy and continue to cook for 5 to 6 minutes.

 

 

Add the mustard and tomato puree and cook for 7 to 8 minutes more, stirring the ingredients together with a spoon.

 

 

Finally, add the onions and 80 gm of smetana cream taken from the total dose (you can use the ‘other part for other preparations, such as accompanying it to Blinis!) and cook another 10 minutes over high heat, until the cream is well blended and dry.

 

 

Finally add the parsley: beef stroganoff is ready to be brought to the table!

 

 

 

The recipe for Beef Stroganoff has several variations including, a very popular one, the addition of champignon mushrooms, to be cooked along with the onions. You can serve your Beef Stroganoff with potatoes, seasonal vegetables or simply with rice pilaf; you can also use other meats such as chicken or veal.

]]> Taiwanese style Shrimps (in Italy called “in Guazzetto”) is a tasty dish, simple and quick to prepare, with aromas and flavors typical of Taiwanese cuisine, rich in spices and seasonings such as soy sauce, rice wine, sesame oil, cilantro (also called “Chinese parsley”) and Thai basil; products that can be found in most well-stocked ethnic stores. This colorful, flavorful and seemingly simple dish is instead a ‘set of interesting, unique and sought-after flavors, perfect as an appetizer, main course or main dish along with Basmati or Jasmine rice.

 

  • 20 gr of fresh ginger root
  • 25 gr of sesame oil (alternatively seed oil)
  • 400 gr fresh shrimp tails
  • 30 gr of rice wine or mirin
  • 20 gr of soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 60 gr leek

 

Shell and clean the shrimp tails well, also removing the black thread inside, and rinse with cold water.

 

 

In a large frying pan, preferably a wok, heat the sesame oil along with the chopped or grated ginger and flavor for 2 minutes, then add the sliced leek and cook another 2 minutes. Add the cleaned shrimp and cook for 2 to 3 minutes, then pour in the rice wine, raise the heat and cook another 3 minutes.

 

 

Add the soy sauce and sugar and cook, still on high heat, for another 4 minutes, allowing some of the cooking liquid to evaporate.

 

 

Serve your Taiwanese-style Shrimp with Basmati rice, perfect for enjoying the savory sauce!

]]> Pan-fried roast veal is a very tender, light, easy-to-prepare, tasty main course, a classic Italian recipe that is usually prepared on Sundays or for a special family meal. With this recipe you will easily get an excellent roast with tender and juicy meat; accompany it with a side of grilled vegetables, baked or mashed potatoes, to enjoy the gravy even better!

 

  • 1 kg of veal for roast (I found it already tied with string at the supermarket!)
  • one onion
  • one carrot
  • 4 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 sprig of rosemary
  • garlic powder one teaspoon
  • 1 glass of white wine
  • 1 glass of broth or water and stock cube
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • one teaspoon of starch

 

If you can’t find the meat already prepared, you need to tie the piece of meat (I recommend walnut, or undercut) with kitchen twine, trying to give it a regular shape. You can prepare this recipe the day before-it will be even tastier.

 

Finely chop the onion and carrot and brown them in a large pot with olive oil, for 5 minutes.

 

Add the rosemary sprig, garlic powder and the veal roast, and brown it well on all sides, so as to seal the meat well, preserving the juices that are inside and keeping it tender while cooking.

 

 

Then add the white wine and deglaze, then turn the roast several times to give it flavor; add the stock or water, salt and pepper and lower the heat to low.

 

Cover the pot with a lid and let the veal roast cook for about an hour and a half, turning it occasionally. After an hour of cooking, raise the heat and let some of the cooking liquid evaporate, with the lid slightly open.

 

Arrange the veal roast on a plate and cut it into slices; remove the rosemary sprig from the pot and blend everything with a food processor, add a teaspoon of starch or cornstarch or flour, stir and return to the heat. Thicken the pan juices until you have a sauce to go with the veal roast, then turn off the heat.

 

 

Place the slices back in the pot, on top of the sauce, and leave them until serving time to make them even tastier!

 

 

Serve the veal roast in slices, accompanied by the sauce and cooked or raw vegetables or mashed potatoes.

]]> Chicken with milk is a velvety, delicate and tasty main course; a quick and easy recipe to prepare, great for Sunday lunch with the family, and also very popular with children. In this recipe the chicken is cooked for a long time in milk, creating a creamy sauce, very pleasant to the palate. Make Chicken in Milk as well: you will bring to the table a wholesome and tasty dish and transform the somewhat dry meat of the chicken into something very moist, flavorful and soft.

 

  • a 1.5 kg chicken in pieces
  • a tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 50 g butter
  • parsley
  • 2 glasses of lukewarm milk
  • extra-virgin olive oil
  • salt and pepper to taste.

 

Clean the chicken pieces by removing the skin; in a large skillet melt the butter, add the chicken pieces and brown them until a crust forms.

 

 

Add the milk, cover with a lid and let it cook on a gentle heat for an hour, season with salt and pepper, then drain the chicken and transfer it to a plate, add the starch to the pan and let it thicken for about 5 minutes until an enveloping sauce has formed.

 

Put the chicken pieces back in and let them season for 5 minutes, finally season with parsley and serve.

Ingredients for two people:

 

  • 2 x 150 g slices of fresh tuna
  • 2 tablespoons chopped oregano
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a tablespoon of flour
  • a small glass of white wine

 

Clean the tuna slices, discard the skin and bones, season them with a tablespoon of oregano, a teaspoon of garlic, a pinch of salt and a tablespoon of oil; cover and let them season for at least an hour in the refrigerator.

 

 

Drain the tuna slices and flour them lightly; wilt the remaining oregano and garlic with a tablespoon of oil in a large skillet, add the tuna and let it brown a few minutes on both sides.

]]> Chinese Meat Patties cone tasty patties with an oriental flavor due to the presence of spices such as ginger and other flavorings as basic ingredients. A popular dish in both China and Taiwan, these patties are prepared with pork, steamed and seasoned with a sauce made from soy sauce and rice vinegar. They can be served in different sizes, from 5 to 10 cm in diameter, and are served in hot soups or topped with this flavorful sauce. If you love oriental flavors also try my Steamed Dumplings and Noodles recipes!

 

  • 10 g fresh ginger
  • 50 gr chopped carrots
  • 30 gr of cooked ham
  • 300 gr chopped pork
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • a teaspoon of sesame oil (alternatively seed oil)
  • 400 gr of water
  • one tablespoon of soy sauce
  • one teaspoon of granulated stock cube
  • 10 gr of cornstarch
  • 20 gr rice vinegar or mirim or sake
  • 2 sliced spring onions

 

With the Bimby: put the ginger in the mug and mince 2 seconds/speed 8, then bring together with the spatula

 

Add the carrots and cooked ham and chop 5 seconds/speed 5

 

 

If you do not have a Bimby, you can use an ordinary food processor.

 

Add the pork, salt, pepper, oil and blend 2 minutes/speed 2

 

Without the Bimby, transfer the meat to a large bowl, add the chopped vegetables and mix the mixture with your hands.

 

 

Form into balls of about 15 g each and spread them on the Varoma dish; if you do not have the Bimby, any steamer will do.

 

Pour the water into the mug, place the Varoma with the meatballs and cook 15 min/Varoma/speed 1. While cooking, mix the rice vinegar with the cornstarch without forming lumps.

 

 

 

Remove the Varoma, add the stock cube and soy sauce to the mug and thicken 2 min/Varoma/speed 2, pouring in the cornstarch with the wine vinegar from the hole in the lid.

 

Add the sliced spring onions and cook 2 minutes/Varoma/speed 1.

 

If you do not have a Bimby, after steaming the meatballs (always 15 minutes) you can prepare the sauce in a small saucepan with the same ingredients and cook until smooth and thick.

 

Transfer the Chinese meatballs to a serving plate, add the thick sauce on top and serve with basmati rice and soy sauce!
